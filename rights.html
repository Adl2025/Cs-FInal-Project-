Know Your Rights

In School

  • You have the right to respect and dignity.
  • You have the right to a safe, supportive environment, free from bullying.
  • You can report bullying anonymously.
  • No one can punish a whole class for one person's actions.
  • You must not insult, harm, or damage property.
What to do if... you see or experience bullying: Tell a trusted adult or use your school's anonymous reporting system. Support the person being bullied and do not join in.

With Police

  • You have the right to know why you are being stopped or questioned.
  • You can ask for a parent or trusted adult to be present if you are under 18.
  • You have the right to remain silent and not answer questions without a lawyer.
  • Be respectful, but you do not have to consent to a search without a reason.
What to do if... you are stopped by police: Stay calm, ask why you are being stopped, and politely request a parent or adult if you are under 18. Do not run or argue.

Online / Cyberbullying

  • You have the right to privacy and to block/report bullies.
  • Do not share personal information or photos without permission.
  • Report harmful messages or threats to a trusted adult or authority.
  • Cyberbullying is taken seriously and can have real consequences.
What to do if... you are bullied online: Block the person, save evidence (screenshots), and report it to a trusted adult or platform. Never respond with anger.
